Cars were invented to provide a more efficient and convenient mode of transportation, replacing horse-drawn carriages. The invention of cars aimed to improve mobility, reduce travel time, and enhance overall transportation capabilities.


Karl Benz - who is known as the father of automobiles, was a German mechanical engineer who designed and in 1885 built the world’s first practical automobile to be powered by an internal-combustion engine. In 1926 the Benz company merged with Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft to form Daimler-Benz, maker of Mercedes-Benz automobiles, a brand soon synonymous with luxury.


Henry Ford, founder of Ford Motor Company in Michigan, USA, built the Model T, is generally regarded as the first mass-affordable automobile, which made car travel available to middle-class Americans. The Ford Model T was introduced to the world in 1908. Henry Ford wanted the Model T to be affordable, simple to operate, and durable. The vehicle was one of the first mass production vehicles, allowing Ford to achieve his aim of manufacturing the universal car that put the world on wheels.


Elon Musk, the founder of Tesla Motors quoted, "Cars give you freedom of travel. You can conveniently go where you want, how you want, and a lot about having a car is about freedom."



Ford

Founded by Henry Ford in 1903, Ford is known for its focus on innovation and affordability. The company pioneered mass production techniques, making cars accessible to the average American. Ford's lineup includes a wide range of vehicles, from the iconic Mustang sports car to the best-selling F-150 pickup truck.
